# Lookalike qualifier

## When this handles a lead
A company surfaced because it resembles good accounts (growth-fit, revenue-fit, or hot lookalikes). It usually has no PostHog usage, so it is qualified on company analysis. But a lookalike can also already be an active account, in which case its real usage is used.

## How it works
Read the CRM record, enrich the named person and the company, and map a candidate use case from company fit. If the lead resolves to a PostHog account, fold in its real usage (events, products, invoices) so the judge weighs activity, not just resemblance. Target the named lead.

## How to judge
If there is no usage, analyze the company and decide whether there is real potential for a sales-led engagement that gets them over $2k MRR quickly; if the fit hypothesis is weak, nurture rather than force a call. If the dossier carries usage (the lookalike turned out to be an active account), weigh that usage heavily; real events, paid invoices, and products in use can make this a clear call regardless of the lookalike label.

## How to draft
Lead with the specific fit hypothesis (why they resemble accounts that do well on PostHog) and the use case it implies, anchored on a grounded company fact. If usage exists, lead with that instead. Keep it light since this is sourced by resemblance, not intent. Soft close.

## How to follow up
One light nudge (7 days), drafts only: a brief follow-up with one fresh fit angle, easy to ignore. A reply stops the sequence; weak fit should nurture rather than keep nudging.

## Notes
Every dossier Claim carries provenance, and the draft is checked at the shell boundary, so no fact that is not grounded in a Claim survives to staging.
