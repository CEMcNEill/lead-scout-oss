# Big fish qualifier

## When this handles a lead
A large-headcount account (500+ or 1000+ employees) active on the free plan with no payment method. The signal is about the account, not the named person.

## How it works
Account-first. Read the CRM record, pull usage and the internal user roster, run buying-group discovery (enrich the roster, segment by role), enrich the company, and map live use cases. Judge holistically with the chosen target taken from the candidate set.

## How to judge
A large company active on the free plan with no payment method. Weight account scale and breadth of usage over firmographic polish. Real usage plus a buyable persona is a call; target the economic buyer or champion from the roster, not the IC who signed up.

## How to draft
Lead with the scale of their own usage and the value they are already getting on the free plan, then the natural next step (a paid plan that fits a company their size, with support and controls). Name a concrete, grounded usage fact. Address the economic buyer or champion, not the IC who signed up. Soft close: offer a short call to map the right plan. No pressure on the free usage itself.

## How to follow up
Two light nudges (5 days, then 9) on the same thread, drafts only, each shorter than the last with one fresh grounded angle. Follow-up 1: a concrete next step (a 15-minute plan-fit call, or a specific product worth a look). Follow-up 2: a low-pressure check-in offering to leave it for now. A reply stops the sequence automatically.

## Notes
The account is the unit. Outreach can correctly go to a different person than the named lead; the disposition records the chosen target and why.
