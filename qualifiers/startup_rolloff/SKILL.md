# Startup-rolloff qualifier

## When this handles a lead
A startup whose program credits are ending, with high credit spend (or >50% of credits used and a last invoice over $5k).

## How it works
Account-first. Read the CRM record, pull usage and the internal user roster, run buying-group discovery (enrich the roster, segment by role), enrich the company, and map live use cases. Judge holistically with the chosen target taken from the candidate set.

## How to judge
A re-evaluation moment, not a disqualifier. Weight the live use case and whether an owner can decide on a paid plan as credits run out.

## How to draft
Lead with the savings opportunity, not just "your credits are ending." Order:
1. Friendly heads-up - credits expire on the known date, after which usage is billed; flag it early so there are no surprises and they have time to decide.
2. The value (the lead): their grown usage likely unlocks a volume discount on a pre-paid plan - frame it as locking in a lower rate vs topping up credits at the standard rate, meaningful annual savings at their scale. A specific discount percentage only if a Claim grounds it; otherwise keep it qualitative and offer specifics on a call.
3. Optional cost-control help: PostHog is usage-based, a little tuning helps - estimating-usage-costs guide, the spend tab, and the AI wizard audit (npx @posthog/wizard@latest audit).
4. Soft close: happy to chat about options if interested. No hard calendar push.

Warm, low-pressure, recipient stays in control. This guidance is carried on the qualifier as `draft_guidance` and handed to the drafter; the fact-check gate still governs every claim.

## Notes
The account is the unit. Outreach can correctly go to a different person than the named lead; the disposition records the chosen target and why.
